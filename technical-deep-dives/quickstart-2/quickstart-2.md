# SEO Metadata and On-Page Content: What Changes After Replatforming

When you change carts and migrate your store data, you can unintentionally change how search engines interpret your pages.

SEO metadata and on-page content continuity is less about one perfect setting and more about protecting the signals that explain what your pages are about.

#### What “SEO metadata” includes

Most stores rely on fields like:

* Page titles and descriptions
* Heading structure on key pages
* Canonical intent (which page should be treated as the primary version)
* Structured content elements that influence relevance and click behavior

Even when these fields transfer, how they are displayed can change because templates and themes differ.

#### What commonly changes after a cart change

Common change patterns include:

* Titles and descriptions present but formatted differently across templates
* Category and product page layouts change, which can change heading structure
* Duplicate content risks increase if collections or filters create multiple similar URLs
* Internal linking patterns change when navigation changes

If URLs change, redirect planning becomes a major part of continuity. For a beginner-friendly explanation, see the URL Redirects page in the SEO and Traffic Continuity section.

#### A beginner-friendly continuity checklist

Prioritize what matters most:

1. Top categories and best sellers\
   Confirm page titles, key headings, and primary content still reflect the intent of the page.
2. High-traffic landing pages\
   Confirm the page still answers the query it used to attract, and the primary message is not lost in a new template.
3. Core trust pages\
   Confirm these pages are reachable and consistent.
4. Internal linking\
   Confirm category to product paths remain logical, and key content still links to relevant commercial pages where appropriate.
