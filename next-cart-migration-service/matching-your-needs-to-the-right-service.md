# Matching Your Needs to the Right Service

Most migration mistakes are not technical. They happen when a team chooses the wrong level of help for their store’s complexity and timeline, then discovers too late that ownership, validation workload, or special requirements were underestimated.

This article helps you translate your situation into the right Next-Cart service choice. It focuses on decision signals you can confirm early in **Demo Migration**, then shows how those signals map to **Standard Migration**, **Managed Migration**, or **Custom Migration**.

### Start with the refined migration journey

Next-Cart migrations follow four stages:

#### Stage 1: Initial Assessment and Demo Migration

You evaluate results using sample data, identify special handling needs, and clarify requirements. You can run the demo yourself or request an expert-assisted demo.

#### Stage 2: Connection Setup and Data Preparation

The connection method depends on platform capabilities. Data may be accessed automatically after connection, or provided as files in some scenarios.

#### Stage 3: Data Mapping

You confirm how store meaning will be represented on the target platform, including product structure, catalog navigation intent, and how customer and order relationships remain usable.

#### Stage 4: Full Migration, Validation, and Recent Data Migration

Full scope is migrated and validated. Recent Data Migration syncs new records added after Full Migration and can be run multiple times.

### A practical way to choose the right service

The most reliable method is to decide based on three questions.

#### 1) How complex is your store, based on evidence

Look for signals from demo results and requirements review:

Low complexity signals:

* mostly standard product structures
* predictable variant behavior in the demo sample
* limited reliance on custom fields or app-stored data
* fewer edge cases in catalog structure

Higher complexity signals:

* variant-heavy catalogs with inconsistent options or attributes used for filtering
* deep or curated categories where browsing intent must be preserved
* heavy dependence on third-party extensions, plugins, or modules
* custom fields that affect storefront display or operations
* requirements like data filtering by criteria
* migration to or from a “Custom Cart” platform

#### 2) How much internal capacity do you have to operate and validate

Ask:

* Who will run the migration tool and manage reruns?
* Who will validate outcomes and sign off?
* Can your team review results quickly enough to keep the timeline realistic?

If execution ownership or validation bandwidth is unclear, risk increases even when the data is straightforward.

#### 3) How strict your proof requirement is before go-live

Projects need stronger proof when:

* SEO and traffic continuity are high stakes
* peak season is near
* promotions and checkout behavior are revenue-critical
* customer support depends on accurate order history

When the cost of surprise is high, selecting a model that reduces execution risk is usually worth it.

### Which service fits which situation

#### Standard Migration is a fit when you want control and your complexity is predictable

Choose **Standard Migration** when:

* demo results for your representative sample look consistent and explainable
* you are comfortable operating the migration tool
* you have clear ownership for validation and sign-off
* your requirements are primarily “core data” and standard behavior

What it gives you:

* you run the migration yourself
* 24/7 experts guide you when questions arise
* you own operation and validation

Standard is often the best match for teams that have time to run tests, reruns, and validation without relying on someone else’s schedule.

#### Managed Migration is a fit when internal capacity is the main risk

Choose **Managed Migration** when:

* you want Next-Cart to execute the migration end-to-end
* internal capacity to operate the process is limited
* you need a more controlled timeline with fewer operational bottlenecks
* you want to focus your time on validating outcomes, not operating migration runs

What it gives you:

* a Next-Cart expert executes the migration process for you
* you primarily validate outcomes
* 24/7 expert support is included

Managed is a strong match when the work is feasible with standard capabilities, but you do not want the project to depend on internal tool-operation bandwidth.

#### Custom Migration is a fit when special requirements must be preserved

Choose **Custom Migration** when demo results or requirements review show that standard capabilities cannot preserve what matters, such as:

* custom fields that drive storefront behavior or filtering
* third-party extension or module data that the business relies on
* non-standard structures that do not translate cleanly
* data filtering requirements that must be implemented consistently
* migration to or from a “Custom Cart” platform

What it gives you:

* one or more **Custom Jobs** adapt the migration tool to the requirement
* a Next-Cart expert executes the migration
* you validate the final result
* 24/7 expert support is included

Custom Migration is best understood as: **Managed Migration plus Custom Jobs**.

### Match your needs using common patterns

Use these patterns as a shortcut after you review demo results.

#### Pattern A: “We mainly need a clean transfer of core data”

Typical needs:

* products, customers, orders, and basic content
* a target store that is structurally correct and usable

Often best fit:

* **Standard Migration** if you can operate and validate internally
* **Managed Migration** if you want the expert to run the migration for you

#### Pattern B: “Our catalog is complex, and discovery must remain strong”

Typical needs:

* complex variants
* attributes used for filtering
* category intent that affects conversion and SEO

Often best fit:

* **Managed Migration** if you need expert execution to keep the flow predictable
* **Custom Migration** if demo results show business-critical fields or behaviors are not preserved without customization

#### Pattern C: “We rely on apps, extensions, or custom fields”

Typical needs:

* plugin-stored data
* custom fields that affect storefront behavior
* third-party module data required for operations

Often best fit:

* **Custom Migration** if standard capabilities cannot handle the requirement

#### Pattern D: “We have a tight timeline and cannot afford execution delays”

Typical needs:

* fewer handoffs
* fewer internal dependencies
* faster progress from assessment to completion

Often best fit:

* **Managed Migration**, unless Custom Jobs are required

#### Pattern E: “We only want a subset of data migrated”

Typical needs:

* migrate only active products
* migrate specific date ranges of orders
* exclude unwanted legacy data

Often best fit:

* depends on criteria and platform pairing
* may require **Custom Migration** if filtering must be implemented as a defined rule set rather than a manual workaround

### What to prepare before choosing a model

You do not need perfect documentation. You do need clarity on what matters.

#### Prepare a representative demo sample

Include:

* best sellers with complex variants
* products that drive filtering and discovery
* top categories and browsing pathways
* a few order scenarios that matter operationally
* a shortlist of any custom fields or app-driven behaviors

#### Define non-negotiable outcomes

Examples:

* variants must behave correctly
* top categories must preserve browsing intent
* order history must remain usable for support
* priority pages must remain reachable and intent-consistent

#### Decide validation ownership

Make it explicit:

* who reviews outcomes
* what “acceptable” means
* what must be fixed versus accepted as a platform difference

**Expert insight**: Most teams choose the wrong service model when they treat validation as an afterthought. Validation workload is the real cost driver. Service fit improves dramatically when you plan validation upfront.

### Conclusion

The right Next-Cart service model is the one that removes your highest risk: operational ownership risk, validation bandwidth risk, or special-requirement risk. Use the Demo Migration stage to surface complexity signals early, then choose Standard Migration when you can operate and validate confidently, Managed Migration when execution capacity is your constraint, and Custom Migration when the tool must be adapted through Custom Jobs to preserve business-critical meaning.

If you want a fast, confident service decision, pick a representative demo sample and list your non-negotiable outcomes, then share 5–10 examples of anything that looks incomplete or business-critical in the results. If you reach out via Live Chat, Next-Cart can help you classify each concern as mapping, scope, platform capability, or Custom Job requirement, and recommend the safest service model for your timeline and store complexity.
